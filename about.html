About Me

Electrical Engineer, Economist, Computer Scientist

I am passionate about integrating business and technology. I have previously worked as an intern at Smith Breeden Associates, where I was exposed to working in a small team to develop a java application to predict the Federal Funds Rate using a customizable model based on a Taylor Rule.

I love using computers to find solutions to challenging problems. I have gained practical problems solving skills from working on a lot of different projects ranging from a spring-mass assembly simulator to a surgical data organization program. These ventures, along with my coursework in electrical computer engineering, economics, and computer science have given me a strong background in quantitative analytics.

I'm also interested in learning about other programming languages. My past experiences in and out of the classroom have focused on teaching me Java and its vast business applications. I am eager to explore ways to become a more flexible coder and express my ideas (which is part of the reason why I'm creating my own website in html, css, and javascript)!

Keep looking through this website to read up on my interests, projects I've worked on, and more!
