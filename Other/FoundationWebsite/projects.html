My Projects

I've worked on a ton of different cool projects both in and outside of the classroom. Here are descriptions of some of the more noteworthy things that I've done.

Oogasalad

I worked in a team of 12 people to create a tower defense game authoring and playing environments in java for my Softawre Design and Implementation course. My focus in the project was the transfer and storage of data from the authoring environment and game engine.

Integrated Design Challenge

I participated in engineering design for a robotics challenge in my Fundamentals for Electrical and Computer Engineering course. The robot I built utilized an Arduino Mega 2560 to follow lines, collect light data via phototransistor, and use an XBee to communicate with other robots.

SLogo

I helped build an Integrated Design Environment in java based on the Logo language. I peer programmed the parser for user input code with a partner while two other team members created the GUI.

Springies

I built a spring-mass assembly simulator using the jgame library. I worked mostly on developing the model for the simulation while my partner focused on parsing Xml files to gather initial environment and object data.

Debugger Game

I built a scolling-shooter game that I named "Debugger." I used the jgame library for the basic GUI design and implemented a rudimentary MVC design pattern for the function of the game. I also explored inheritance hierarchies for objects through this project.

Singh-Jo Rule

I peer programmed an application that would use a variant of the Taylor Rule to generate extrapolations of the Federal Funds Rate based off of CPI, PPI, GDPPOT, Real GDP, and other variables.

My Publications

MONS Research

I was a mentor of a summer research program called Mars Outreach for North Carolina Students (MONS). Through MONS, I was able to teach students how to design and conduct an experiment applicable to the study of Mars. After performing research over the course of a year, I was able to publish an abstract and present my team's findings at the 43rd Lunar and Planetary Science Conference.
